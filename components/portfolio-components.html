Hanz and Franz

Hanz and Franz began as a practice in manipulating the DOM, or specifically a progress bar. Once I had the simple JavaScript code written for a one player game, I decided to take it to the next level by including an additional player.
Each player has two attack modes and two defenses. Later I decided the second player shouldn't have to control the defensive moves. So I wrote a random function that has two defenses and two non-reactive responses. Depending on the attack and defense, the health of the players varies.
I began with simple alerts. Unsatisfied with this solution, I designed a vintage sideshow with two humorous fighters.
At first I researched sprites. Once I had several put together and included in my game, I realized I didn't like the way they worked or looked. So I did more research and learned to create gifs. Then I simply had to figure out how to get a gif to work "once" on command. That turned out to be pretty difficult. I finally figured out how to replace the gif with a png each time an attack was triggered. Then I had to set a delay so that defense didn't happen at the exact same time.
I carefully placed each of the 10 gifs and pngs, so hopefully there wouldn't be alot of movement.
I hope you enjoy Hanz and Franz. My favorite giff is when Franz is knocked-out.

Pop-Up Devil

I took the "whack-a-mole" game and switched it up a bit. I made a series of wind-up "Jack-in-the-box" using Photoshop. There are three colors- red, blue, and green. Each color has the possibility of launching a monkey or a devil.
As the boxes play their music and transition between four winding images, the player can click on any box. The question is- will they find the monkey or the devil?
This game is still under construction.
